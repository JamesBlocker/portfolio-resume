Recent Projects

Etches - Natural Language Processing

  • Etches.herokuapp.com/
  • GitHub/Etches

Etch is a web app that utilizes the IBM’s Watson, the super computer, API to accurately analyze language in context and reveal underlying emotions. The idea was originally to provide a service for those with social disabilities such Asperger Syndrome where sometimes what is communicated does not line up with the intent due to overlooked social cues and norms. By contextually analyzing text we can identify primary emotions being broadcast such as fear, joy, and anger.

We quickly learned that the app was fun to use analyzing all manner of text. Soon users were checking out their favorite song lyrics, poems, and passages from books. The results were generally very surprising and fun. The app was built as a 4 person team at General Assembly, Team Sherlock, and I was lead app designer and in charge of the API design and visual design. The app was featured in our schools “Best Of” newsletter to alumni and prospective students.

Neighborhood Good - Community Portal

  • Neighborhood-Good.herokuapp.com/
  • GitHub/Neighborhood-Good

Neighborhood Good is a community portal for the city of Glendora, CA. A place for residents to share community events and posts of interest to the city. It is a simplified Facebook of sorts with a tight focus on building a better local community. The page allows users to browse posts, create and account, create/edit posts.

This community portal was built using a full MERN stack (Mongo DB database, Express back-end, React front-end, and executed with Node JS). The site has full authentication and is encrypted with bcrypt and is easily extended to cater to other communities. The site comes with a full host of city links to keep the community informed of upcoming votes, available after school programs, and city meetings.
